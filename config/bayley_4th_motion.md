# Gross Motor Subtest
## Item 36: Walks Series: Without Support
* **Materials:** None
* **Related Item:** 46
* **Item Instructions:** If the child is not walking freely, have the caregiver stand the child on the floor and hold the child's hands so that the child's arms are extended in front at shoulder height.
Encourage the child to walk toward the caregiver. If the child walks with coordinated steps, have the caregiver slowly release grasp on the child's hands according to the child's ability to walk unsupported.
* **Scoring Criteria & Scores:**
  * 2: Takes at least 5 steps without support with good coordination and without using arms for balance.
  * 1: Takes at least 3 steps without support but without good coordination and uses arms for balance (e.g., medium or high guard).
  * 0: Does not take at least 3 steps without support, gait is asymmetric, or does not attempt to walk.

## Item 37: Throws Ball Forward
* **Materials:** Small ball
* **Related Item:** None
* **Item Instructions:** Using an overhand motion, throw the ball gently so that it lands in front of the child. Allow him or her to retrieve it. If the child does not retrieve the ball, hand the ball to the child and encourage him or her to throw it. If the child does not throw the ball, retrieve it, throw it to the child again, and urge him or her to throw it back.
* **Scoring Criteria & Scores:**
  * 2: Purposely throws ball forward using either an underhand or overhand motion. | Almost every time
  * 1: Attempts to throw ball forward using either an underhand or overhand motion but ball goes straight up or backward. | Some of the time
  * 0: Drops or rolls ball. | None of the time
* **Caregiver Question:** Is [insert child's name] able to successfully throw a ball forward almost every time [he/she] tries, some of the time, or none of the time? [ ]

## Item 38: Unsupported Squat
* **Materials:** Object of interest
* **Related Item:** None
* **Item Instructions:** While the child is standing, place an object on the floor and encourage the child to pick it up.
* **Scoring Criteria & Scores:**
  * 2: Smoothly moves from standing to squatting to standing while maintaining balance without using any support.
  * 1: Moves from standing to squatting to standing but may stumble a bit or need slight support, such as stabilizing with 1 hand on floor.
  * 0: Sits down to retrieve object or needs support to pick up object.

## Item 40: Walks Backward
* **Materials:** None
* **Related Item:** 51
* **Item Instructions:** Demonstrate walking backward and encourage the child to walk backward alongside you. Encourage him or her to continue taking steps backward. The caregiver can hold the child's hand initially.
* **Scoring Criteria & Scores:**
  * 2: Takes at least 3 steps backward without support and shows good balance.
  * 1: Takes 2 steps backward without support; slight loss of balance acceptable.
  * 0: Does not take at least 2 steps backward without support or walks backward and loses balance after 1 to 2 steps.

## Item 43: Balances on Each Foot With Support
* **Materials:** Timer
* **Related Item:** None
* **Item Instructions:** 
  * Trial 1 : Stand beside the child and hold 1 hand. Ask the child to lift his or her left foot. You may demonstrate by lifting your left foot or pointing as you encourage the child.
  * Trial 2 : Administer Trial 2 in the same manner as Trial 1, asking the child to lift his or her right foot.
* **Scoring Criteria & Scores:**
  * 2: Balances on both left and right foot with support for at least 2 seconds.
  * 1: Balances on only right or only left foot with support for at least 2 seconds.
  * 0: Does not balance on either foot for at least 2 seconds or balances on either foot but requires support beyond just holding child's hand.

## Item 45: Kicks Ball
* **Materials:** Large ball
* **Related Item:** None
* **Item Instructions:** Place the ball on the floor. Gently swing your foot behind you and then out in front to kick the ball. Retrieve the ball and place it in front of the child. Encourage the child to kick the ball, reminding him or her to kick gently. The child may attempt to kick the ball up to 3 times.
* **Scoring Criteria & Scores:**
  * 2: Maintains balance while kicking ball in a forward direction at least 2 ft.
  * 1: Kicks ball, but it moves forward less than 2 ft or child loses balance in the process of kicking.
  * 0: Makes kicking motion but misses ball, or kicks ball but requires support to maintain balance.

## Item 46: Walks on Path: Forward
* **Materials:** Stepping path
* **Related Item:** 36
* **Item Instructions:** Place the stepping path on the floor. Walk forward along its entire length, taking small strides and placing both feet on the stepping path. Say, Now you try. Remind the child to keep both feet on the path.
* **Scoring Criteria & Scores:**
  * 2: Takes at least 2 steps with both feet on stepping path and keeps 1 foot on path for at least 5 ft.
  * 1: Walks for at least 5 ft and keeps at least 1 foot on stepping path.
  * 0: Does not keep 1 foot on stepping path or does not walk a distance of at least 5 ft.

## Item 47: Jumps Forward Series: 6 Inches
* **Materials:** Stepping path
* **Related Item:** 48, 58
* **Item Instructions:** 
  * Trial 1 : Place the stepping path on the floor. Point to the beginning of the stepping path and say, Stand right here and jump as far as you can. Make sure that the child's feet are together and at the beginning of the stepping path. You may demonstrate this item a maximum of 3 times. The child may jump with feet together or lead with 1 foot, as long as both feet are in the air at some point during the jump.
  * Trials 2-3 : For each trial, say, Do it again. See how far you can jump.
* **Scoring Criteria & Scores:**
  * 2: Jumps at least 6 in. for any trial.
  * 1: Jumps 1 to 5 in. for any trial.
  * 0: Does not jump any distance for any trial.

## Item 48: Jumps: Both Feet
* **Materials:** None
* **Related Item:** 47, 48, 58
* **Item Instructions:** While the child is standing, take a small jump and say, Watch me jump. Now it's your turn.
* **caregiver question:** Ask the caregiver, Is [insert child's name] able to jump off the floor with both feet in the air at the same time almost every time [he/she] tries, some of the time, or none of the time?
* **Scoring Criteria & Scores:**
  * 2: Jumps off floor with feet together or leads with 1 foot and both feet are in the air at some point during the jump. | Almost every time
  * 1: Jumps but only 1 foot is in the air. | Some of the time
  * 0: Bends legs in an attempt to jump, but both feet remain in contact with floor. | None of the time
* **Caregiver Question:** Is [insert child's name] able to jump off the floor with both feet in the air at the same time almost every time [he/she] tries, some of the time, or none of the time? [ ]

## Item 50: Walks on Tiptoes
* **Materials:** Stepping path
* **Related Item:** None
* **Item Instructions:** Place the stepping path on the floor.  Walk on your tiptoes along the entire length of the stepping path as you say, See? I'm walking on my tiptoes. You do it. Tiptoe all the way down this line.
The child does not have to stay on the stepping path to receive credit.
* **Scoring Criteria & Scores:**
  * 2: Takes at least 4 steps without touching heels to floor.
  * 1: Takes 1 to 3 steps without touching heels to floor.
  * 0: Does not take any steps without touching heels to floor.

## Item 51: Walks on Path: Backward
* **Materials:** Stepping path
* **Related Item:** 40
* **Item Instructions:** Place the stepping path on the floor. Walk backward for the entire length of the stepping path, taking small strides and placing both feet on the stepping path.(heel to toe) Say, Now it's your turn. Remind the child to keep both feet on the path.
* **Scoring Criteria & Scores:**
  * 2: Walks backward within approximately 6 in. of stepping path for at least 5 ft.
  * 1: Walks backward close to path for several steps.
  * 0: Stumbles walking backward, requires support, or cannot walk backward.
